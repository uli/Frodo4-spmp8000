Overview

"Ú-queta i yéni avánier alye,
ú-queta i cirya ná vanwa!"
Frodo is an open and free C64 emulator for BeOS, Unix, MacOS, AmigaOS, Win32 and RiscOS systems and the world's first C64 emulator not bearing a "64" in its name. :-)

Frodo comes in two flavours: The regular Frodo with a line-based emulation, and the single-cycle emulation Frodo SC that is slower but far more compatible.

In addition to a precise 6510/VIC emulation, Frodo features a full, processor-level 1541 emulation that is able to handle most fast loaders. There is also an alternative, faster 1541 emulation of four drives in .d64/x64 disk images, .t64/LYNX archives, or directories of the host system.

Frodo runs on a variety of platforms. The "core" distribution of Frodo available from the Frodo homepage is targeted at

  • Unix systems with SDL/OpenGL, X11R6 or Linux/SVGAlib
Other platforms supported by the core distribution, but no longer actively maintained, include:

  • Intel x86 systems running Windows NT/95
  • BeOS-ready systems (PPC or x86) with BeOS R3 or higher
  • 68k or PPC Macintosh with System 7.5
  • Amiga/DraCo with 68040/68060, AmigaOS 3.0 and a graphics card (AHI V3 required for sound)
  • Acorn computers with RiscOS 3
Ports of Frodo to other platforms, including Mac OS X, mobile devices, and gaming consoles, are maintained by independent developer groups. Most of these are linked to from the Frodo homepage.
